Executive Summary

A pristine, show-ready townhome that's hard to sell today — and easy to rent right now.

Your end-unit townhome has sat on the sale market for 147 days, through $25,000 in price reductions from $465,000 to $440,000 — and even at today's list it sits roughly $41,800 below the $481,800 you paid in February 2024, before the 6–8% in selling costs a sale would carry. In a market still crowded with builder inventory and resales, selling now most likely means locking in a loss.

Meanwhile, the rental side of this same community is moving briskly: comparable Tyson Ranch townhomes have leased in 16 to 37 days at $2,500 to $2,800, your HOA permits leasing with no waiting period and no approval required, and your home — a 2023 end unit with a pond view and no rear neighbors — sits at the top of that peer set. Renting turns a stalled sale into monthly income, and keeps your option to sell open for a stronger day. This analysis lays out both paths so the decision is yours, with clear numbers in hand.
